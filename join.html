Join Terra Reform

Terra Reform welcomes motivated students interested in science, engineering, software development, research, and sustainability. We are an international initiative and welcome students from all backgrounds, disciplines, and countries.

Who We're Looking For

Find Your Place

Whether you're passionate about clean energy, robotics, accessibility, or scientific research — there's a place for you here.

Researchers

Students who enjoy investigating open questions, designing experiments, and writing up findings — physics, materials science, environmental science, and beyond.

Engineers & Builders

Students who like designing and prototyping hardware — from robotics platforms to communication devices and educational kits.

Developers

Students who can build software, simulations, and tools — including websites, applications, and interactive learning environments.

Sustainability Advocates

Students focused on environmental and societal challenges, from clean energy systems to community resilience.

Educators & Communicators

Students interested in making science and technology more accessible — through teaching, content, and outreach.

Designers

Students who care about accessibility and inclusive design — making sure our projects work for everyone.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Who can join?

Any motivated student — primarily high school and early university — from any country, school, or background. There are no grade requirements; curiosity and commitment matter more than credentials.

Does it cost anything?

No. Terra Reform is a non-commercial, student-led initiative. Joining and participating is completely free.

Do I need prior experience?

No. Many of our projects started with members learning the necessary skills along the way. If you already have experience in research, coding, design, or hardware — great — but willingness to learn is the only real requirement.

How much time does it take?

It depends on the project and the phase — competition deadlines are busier, other periods are relaxed. Most members contribute a few hours a week, scheduled around school. We plan around your availability, not the other way around.

Is it remote or in person?

Almost everything happens online, which is what makes international collaboration possible. Occasional in-person opportunities (competitions, events) depend on the project and where you live.

Can I propose my own project idea?

Absolutely — some of our best work started as a member's idea. If you have a concept that fits our mission, we'll help you develop it, find teammates, and target the right program or competition.
