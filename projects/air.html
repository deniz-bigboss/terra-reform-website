Autonomous Infantry Robot (AIR)

A humanoid robotics concept exploring remote operation and future autonomous functionality for deployment in dangerous environments, with applications in search and rescue, hazardous site inspection, and emergency response.

●  Concept Development · TEKNOFEST

Overview

The Autonomous Infantry Robot (AIR) project explores the design and operational architecture of a humanoid robot intended for use in environments that are too dangerous or inaccessible for human operators. The concept begins with remote operation — a human operator controls the robot from a safe distance using real-time telemetry and sensor feeds — and lays groundwork for future incremental autonomy.

The humanoid form factor is a deliberate choice: human environments — collapsed buildings, flooded corridors, uneven terrain — are designed for human bodies. A robot that can navigate these spaces without extensive environmental modification has a natural operational advantage in real emergency scenarios.

Key Aspects

  • Humanoid robot platform design with focus on bipedal or multi-limbed locomotion
  • Remote operation system architecture with real-time video and sensor telemetry
  • Hazardous environment navigation — rubble, unstable terrain, confined spaces
  • Search and rescue application scenarios: locating survivors in disaster zones
  • Hazardous material handling and inspection without human exposure
  • Foundation architecture designed to support incremental autonomous decision-making

Application Scenarios

The primary intended use cases for AIR are environments where human presence creates unacceptable risk. Collapsed buildings following earthquakes, sites with chemical or radiological hazards, and active fires are examples where sending a remotely operated humanoid robot instead of a human responder could save lives — both the lives of potential victims the robot locates, and the lives of rescue workers who would otherwise be exposed.

The project does not claim current autonomous capability. The concept is grounded in remote operation as the near-term goal, with autonomous features considered as a research direction for future development phases.

Program Context

Developed for TEKNOFEST's robotics and autonomous systems competition track, where student teams design, build, or conceptually develop robotic systems across a range of application domains. The competition encourages ambitious technical thinking and engineering-focused project development.
