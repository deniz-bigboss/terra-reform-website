TEKNOFEST Safety Communication

Safe Explorer

A portable emergency communication concept designed for outdoor sports and remote environments, using radio-based communication to improve safety, emergency response, and rescue coordination.

●  Concept Development · TEKNOFEST

Overview

Safe Explorer addresses a critical gap in outdoor safety infrastructure: the lack of reliable communication in remote environments. When mountaineers, hikers, cyclists, and skiers venture into areas beyond mobile network coverage, their ability to call for help in an emergency is severely limited.

The project proposes a portable device and network concept that uses radio-based communication to link individual users in the field with central coordination stations. This creates a coverage layer that operates independently of cellular infrastructure, functioning in mountain terrain, dense forests, and other remote locations where conventional communication breaks down.

Key Aspects

  • Radio-based peer-to-peer and hub-to-station communication architecture
  • Emergency alert broadcasting with location data
  • Portable, weather-resistant hardware concept for field use
  • Central coordination station design for search and rescue teams
  • Low-power operation to extend battery life in multi-day expeditions
  • Target users: mountaineers, hikers, cyclists, skiers, outdoor adventurers

Problem Statement

Outdoor sports accidents frequently result in fatalities not because rescue is impossible, but because the distress signal never reaches rescuers in time — or at all. Standard mobile phones fail in areas with no cell coverage. Satellite communicators exist but remain expensive and inaccessible for most recreational users.

Safe Explorer aims to create a middle-ground solution: more capable than a simple whistle or mirror signal, more accessible than satellite systems, and purpose-built for the communication patterns of outdoor emergency response.

Program Context

Developed for TEKNOFEST, Turkey's largest technology and aerospace festival, which hosts competitive tracks for student engineering and technology projects across a wide range of disciplines.
