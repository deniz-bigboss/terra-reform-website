TÜBİTAK 2204-A Education Software

Virtual Physics Laboratory

A Roblox-based interactive virtual physics laboratory designed to make practical science education more accessible through interactive simulations covering multiple physics topics. Regional Finalist in the TÜBİTAK 2204-A High School Research Projects Competition.

●  Regional Finalist · TÜBİTAK 2204-A

Overview

The Virtual Physics Laboratory is an interactive simulation environment built on the Roblox platform, designed to give students access to physics experiments that may not be available in under-resourced schools. By using Roblox — a platform that millions of students already have access to — the project removes the barrier of specialized software or expensive hardware while still providing meaningful experimental experiences.

The laboratory covers a range of physics topics through interactive simulations, allowing students to conduct experiments, observe results, and engage with physical principles in a hands-on way even when a physical laboratory is unavailable. The project became a Regional Finalist in the TÜBİTAK 2204-A High School Students Research Projects Competition.

Key Aspects

  • Built on the Roblox platform for maximum accessibility — no specialized software required
  • Interactive simulations across multiple physics domains: mechanics, optics, electricity, and more
  • Designed to complement and supplement in-person laboratory work
  • Student-friendly interface aimed at secondary school physics curricula
  • Scalable distribution — accessible to any student with a Roblox account
  • Regional Finalist, TÜBİTAK 2204-A High School Students Research Projects Competition

Motivation

Physics education has a persistent hands-on deficit. In many schools, physical laboratories are underfunded, overcrowded, or simply absent. Students who never perform a real experiment struggle to develop the intuition and practical understanding that theoretical teaching alone cannot provide.

By choosing Roblox as the platform, the project acknowledges where students already are — and meets them there. The result is a laboratory experience that is free, widely accessible, and engaging, rather than isolated to well-funded institutions.

Competition Context

TÜBİTAK 2204-A is the national high school students research projects competition organized by The Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey (TÜBİTAK). The competition encourages original research and innovation by high school students across Turkey. The Virtual Physics Laboratory project achieved Regional Finalist status in this competition.
