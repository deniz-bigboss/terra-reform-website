Solar-Powered School Microgrid

A sustainability-focused proposal investigating how solar-powered microgrids deployed at schools can provide clean energy, serve as educational STEM hubs, and build community resilience.

●  Research Proposal · NYAS Global STEM Alliance

Overview

The Solar-Powered School Microgrid project was developed as part of the New York Academy of Sciences (NYAS) Global STEM Alliance challenge. The proposal investigates how schools in underserved or grid-unstable regions could benefit from locally installed solar photovoltaic generation systems paired with battery storage and intelligent energy management.

Beyond simply providing electricity, the concept positions the school microgrid as a dual-purpose infrastructure: a source of reliable, clean energy for the school and surrounding community, and a living STEM laboratory where students interact directly with real energy systems as part of their education.

Key Aspects

  • Solar PV generation and grid-scale battery storage system design
  • Forecast-driven energy management using weather and demand prediction
  • Educational STEM laboratory integration — students monitor and engage with live energy data
  • Community resilience hub concept — the school serves as a reliable power source during grid outages
  • Student-operated energy monitoring and reporting systems
  • Assessment of real-world deployment feasibility and cost-benefit analysis

Motivation

Access to reliable electricity remains a significant barrier to educational quality in many parts of the world. Schools that face frequent outages or have no grid connection at all cannot run computers, laboratories, or modern equipment effectively. Solar microgrids offer a scalable, decentralized solution that could directly improve educational outcomes while contributing to broader sustainability goals.

Simultaneously, having students operate, monitor, and study a real energy system on their own school grounds transforms the learning experience — turning abstract physics and engineering concepts into tangible, lived understanding.

Program Context

This project was developed through the New York Academy of Sciences (NYAS) Global STEM Alliance, an international program connecting student innovators to work on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics challenges with real-world relevance.
