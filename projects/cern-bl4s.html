CERN BL4S Physics Research

CERN BL4S Project

A proposed scientific research project submitted to the CERN Beamline for Schools competition, investigating lead shielding configurations to protect NbN superconducting thin films from proton radiation damage.

●  Research Proposal · CERN Beamline for Schools

Overview

This project was developed as a submission to the CERN Beamline for Schools (BL4S) competition, which invites high school students worldwide to propose experiments using a real particle beam at CERN. The proposal investigates a specific and technically demanding question: how can niobium nitride (NbN) superconducting thin films be protected from proton radiation damage using lead shielding?

NbN superconducting thin films are materials with significant potential in quantum technologies, particle physics instrumentation, and space applications. However, exposure to proton radiation — as encountered in particle accelerator environments and in space — degrades their superconducting properties. Understanding and mitigating this damage is relevant to making these materials more practical in real-world applications.

Key Aspects

  • Investigation of lead shielding configurations and their effectiveness against proton radiation
  • Analysis of proton-induced damage mechanisms in NbN superconducting thin films
  • Proposed use of CERN proton beam infrastructure for controlled irradiation experiments
  • Applications in space technology — radiation hardening of superconducting components
  • Implications for particle physics detector development and quantum technology systems
  • Exploration of shielding geometry, thickness, and material composition variables

Scientific Context

Superconducting thin films like NbN are used in single-photon detectors, quantum computing components, and sensitive scientific instruments. Their vulnerability to radiation is a known engineering challenge in both space applications and high-energy physics environments. While established techniques exist for shielding classical electronics, the specific behaviour of superconducting materials under proton bombardment — and the effectiveness of lead shielding at various thicknesses — requires dedicated experimental investigation.

This project represents Terra Reform's most technically ambitious research direction, engaging directly with open questions at the intersection of materials science, particle physics, and quantum technology.

Competition Context

CERN Beamline for Schools is an annual international competition hosted by CERN that invites teams of high school students to propose experiments using a real CERN particle beam. The competition is one of the most prestigious student science competitions in the world, encouraging students to think and work like professional experimental physicists.
