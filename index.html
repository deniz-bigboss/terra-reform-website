Who We Are

An Independent Student-Led Innovation Initiative

Terra Reform brings together ambitious students who want to move beyond discussing challenges and instead design, build, and test real solutions. We are not a company, not a school club — we are an independent, international student-led initiative focused on doing serious work.

Our work spans scientific research, hardware engineering, software development, and policy — always grounded in evidence and shaped by a commitment to measurable, real-world impact.

Mission

To empower students to transform ideas into impactful projects that create positive change in society.

Vision

To become a globally connected student innovation network where young people collaborate across disciplines to develop solutions for environmental, technological, educational, and societal challenges — evolving into an innovation ecosystem and startup incubator for student-led solutions.

What Makes Us Different

Terra Reform combines science, engineering, software, sustainability, and international collaboration. Members research, design, build, test, and present real projects — not just discuss problems.

Core Values

Innovation Scientific Thinking Engineering Excellence Sustainability Collaboration Leadership Accessibility Continuous Learning Global Perspective Real-World Impact

Our Work

Projects

Research, engineering, and software projects developed through national and international competitions and independent initiatives. Projects are presented as they are — competition entries, research proposals, concept developments, and simulations.

Sustainability · Energy

Solar-Powered School Microgrid

A sustainability-focused proposal investigating solar generation, battery storage, and forecast-driven energy management to create clean energy hubs serving schools and surrounding communities as resilience anchors.

NYAS Learn More
Safety · Communication

Safe Explorer

A portable emergency communication concept for outdoor sports and remote environments. The system uses radio-based communication between users and central stations to improve safety, emergency response, and rescue coordination for mountaineers, hikers, cyclists, and skiers.

TEKNOFEST Learn More
Accessibility · Education

Braille Circuits

An accessibility-focused educational project that uses tactile Braille-based representations to help visually impaired individuals understand electrical circuits and engage meaningfully with STEM education through inclusive design.

TEKNOFEST Learn More
Education · Software

Virtual Physics Laboratory

A Roblox-based interactive virtual physics laboratory designed to make practical science education more accessible. Covers multiple physics topics through interactive simulations. Regional Finalist in the TÜBİTAK 2204-A High School Research Projects Competition.

TÜBİTAK 2204-A Learn More
Physics · Research

CERN BL4S Project

A proposed scientific research project investigating lead shielding methods to protect NbN superconducting thin films from proton radiation, with potential applications in space technologies, particle physics, and quantum systems.

CERN BL4S Learn More
Robotics · Engineering

Autonomous Infantry Robot (AIR)

A humanoid robotics concept exploring remote operation and future autonomous functionality for deployment in dangerous environments, with applications in search and rescue, hazardous site inspection, and emergency response.

Competitions & Programs

Where We've Competed

Terra Reform has participated in national and international competitions and programs across science, technology, engineering, and policy.

01

TEKNOFEST

Turkey's largest technology and aerospace festival. Terra Reform has developed multiple engineering and technology projects — including Safe Explorer, Braille Circuits, and AIR — for TEKNOFEST competitions.

02

TÜBİTAK

High school research project competitions organized by The Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey. Virtual Physics Laboratory was a Regional Finalist in the TÜBİTAK 2204-A competition.

03

CERN Beamline for Schools

An international competition hosted by CERN inviting high school students to propose experiments using a real CERN particle beam, encouraging advanced physics and experimental design thinking.

04

New York Academy of Sciences

A Global STEM Alliance challenge series connecting student innovators internationally. Terra Reform developed the Solar-Powered School Microgrid project as part of the NYAS program.

05

Büyük Ankara AI & Climate Policy Workshop

A policy-oriented program exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence, climate change, and governance — connecting student thinkers with real policy challenges.
